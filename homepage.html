The History of India

The History of India begins with Indus and the Aryans. This time period was known as the pre-Vedic and Vedic periods. The Indus Valley Civilization rose alongside Mesopotamia and was created to access rivers. Along Vedas which was the next era of civilization which was a time of growth for Hinduism. The Buddist Era which was led by Sakyas of Kapilavastu and the Licchavis of Vaishali. During this period, Buddhism was founded and based on the ideas of spirituality. These eras came to an end due to the invasion led by Alexander in 326 BC, but then began the Golden Age of India. The Gupta Dynasty was known as the Golden age of India which helped India grow politically and economically. This led to the last of the kingdoms in India, Harshavardhana who ruled India after his brothers died, but was defeated by another kingdom ending this kingdom. Medieval India began with The Chalukyas, The Pallavas, The Pandyas, The Rashtrakutas, The Cholas. All these dynasties played a crucial role in helping India grow. With the help of many people, India was able to grow into a beautiful country that is a home for many people.

India Now

As of 2022, India is home to one of the world's largest populations, housing around 1.4 billion people. The current Prime Minister of the country is Narendra Modi, the former governer of Uttar Pradesh, a state in Northern India.

The two main religious sects in India are Hindus and Muslims, though Christians, Buddhists, etc., have a presence in the country. Recently, conflicts between these groups has led to increased religious tension in India.
