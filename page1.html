Hinduism and Islam are the two major religions of India.

Hinduism

Hinduism is considered to be the oldest religion in the world, having roots and customs that can be traced back more than 4000 years. It is the world's third largest religion, and is primarily followed in India, with almost 80% of India's population being Hindu. In Hinduism, many dieties are worshipped and respected, but only the Brahman is considerd to be the supreme power. Hindu paces of worship are called temples, and many can be found all over India, and even other countries One of the most revered temples of Hinduism is the Badrinath Temple located in the town of Uttarkhand.

Islam

Scholars typically date the creation of Islam back to the 7th century, making it the youngest of the major world religions. Islam is believed to have been started in Mecca (modern-day Saudi Arabia). It is a monotheistic religion centered around the Quran, an Islamic religous text. It is the world's second largest religion, and is the world's fastest growing religous group. In India, 14.2% of the population is Muslim, making it India's second-largest religion. The Islamic place of worship is called a Mosque, an important one being Masjid al-Haram in Mecca.
