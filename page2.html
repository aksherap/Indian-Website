India is home to eight classical dances, all of which can be traced back to the Natya Shastra, an ancient sanskrit treatise on the performing arts.

Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is a major Indian classical dance form that originated in Tamil Nadu. It expresses South Indian religious themes, particularily Hinduism. It incorporates facial expressions, hand gestures, and complex footwork. It is the oldest classical dance tradition in India and has been performed in temples, royal courts, and today, everywhere.

Kuchipudi

Kuchipudi is another one of the 8 Indian classical dances of India. It originated from a village named Kuchipudi in the Indian state of Andra Pradesh. Like Bharatanatyam, Kuchipudi is a dance-drama performance with roots in the Natya Shastra and Hinduism. It developed as a largely Krishna-oriented tradition. The typical instruments used in Kuchipudi are mridangam, cymbals, veena, flute, and the tambura. An interetsing aspect of this dance is the performing of a tarangam, a specific dance piece in which the dancer dances upon a brass plate.

Odissi

Odissi is an ancient Indian classical dance that orginated in the temples of Odisha, an eastern coastal state of India. Also found on these temples are sculptures of Odissi dancers. The dance is used to convey religious ideas and stories found in Hindusim. Like the other classical dances, Odissi finds its roots in the Natya Shastra. Odissi is perfomed as a composite of basic dance motif called the Bhangas: symmetric body bends and stance.

Mohiniyattam

Mohiniyattam is a classical dance form that developed in the Indian state of Kerala. The name of the dance is derived from the name "Mohini," an avatar of Lord Krishna. The dance's roots are in the Natya Shastra, and it follows the "Lasya" style detailed in the scripture, meaning the movements are delicate and feminine. This dance has primarily been a Vaishnava tradition, meaning it was danced in worship of Lord Vishnu, and the earliest record of this dance form can be found in an 11th century Vishnu temple.

Kathakali

Kathakali is a major Indian classical dance form originating in the Indian state of Kerala. It is distinguished by its elaborate and colorful costumes and makeup. Unlike other classical dances, Kathakali utilizes movements from ancient Indian martial arts and athletic traditions. The traditional themes conveyed through Kathakali performances are folk stories, religious legends, and spiritual ideas from Hinduism.

Sattriya

Sattriya is an ancient classical dance that originated in Assam, a northeastern state in India. The dance form performs themes that relate to Lord Krishna, Rama, Vishnu, and other important Hindu figues. It was originally created as part of Indian one-act plays, but now have evolved to be performed worldwide.

Manipuri

Manipuri, also referred to as Manipura Raas Leela, orginates from the state of Manipur. The dance performs themes relating to Madhura-Raas and Radha-Krishna,important figures in the Hindu religion. It is a portrayal of the divine love found between Radha and Krishna. The roots of this dance are found in the Natyra Shastra well as cultural fusion with various folk dances.

Kathak

Kathak is an Indian classical dance form that originates in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. The origin of Kathak is traditionally attributed to traveling bards of Ancient India, Kathakers, or , storytellers. Kathak dancers tell various stories through complex foot and handwork, and are most known for the complicated and impressive spins found in the dance form.
